Institutionalized Racism

What is Institutionalized Racism?

The Oxford Dictionary definition of institutionalized racism is racial discrimination that has become established as normal behaviour within a society or organization. Institutionalized racism is a major problem in American society. There are clear injustices happening in America, however we tend to turn a blind eye to them. Predominantly, racism occurs in the justice system and education.

In the Justice System

Policies that are made to slight minorities are made and enforced everyday. For example, New York's Stop and Frisk policy is heavily unfavorable to minorites. From 2003 to 2016, African-Americans and Latinos counted for about 85 percent of the Stop and Frisk searches in New York City. In this timeframe, the number of searches peaked at 685,724 in 2011, however the number of searches has been going down since then. There were only 12,404 searches in 2016. In Education



Only recently in American history have minorities had the same rights to an education as their white counterparts. The Brown v. Board of Education case was only 60 years ago. That's less than one lifetime ago. Minorities have had limited access to higher education in the form of colleges and universities. Historically Black Colleges and Universities, such as Howard University and Morehouse College, have helped the black community get equal access to higher education. However, the problem doesn't end with equal access. In 2015, there were 146 reported cases of racial harassment on college campuses. This number is not a true portrayal of the amount of racial harassment that takes places on college campuses because not all cases get reported. The Higher Education Research Institute of UCLA estimated that only 13% of racial incidents at colleges get reported. That means tthere is a potential 977 cases that went unreported in 2015. Policies and acts, such as affirmative action and Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 have helped minorities gain equal footing in a society driven by money.
